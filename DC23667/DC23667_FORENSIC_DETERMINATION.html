Final Determination

Finding 1 — Conflict of Interest (Brian Lutchmiah)

Brian Lutchmiah, as DIS Manager, initiated the ULIP support offer through Angele Venchard on or after 1 February 2024. He subsequently sat, without recusal, on the Stage 2 panel that declared the student had failed to act on that exact item. This constitutes a direct conflict of interest in the decision-making process. The finding on Action Plan item 3 is tainted.

Finding 2 — Prior Knowledge (Salome Verrell)

Salome Verrell was CC'd on three support and safeguarding-related emails in November and December 2023. She had informal contact with the student. She sent him a group email on 26 April 2024. The claim that the student "did not liaise with his PSL" is directly contradicted by her own correspondence record. The liaison claim is unsustainable.

Finding 3 — Duty of Care Breach (Megan Liskevich)

The Mental Health Advisor sat on a panel 20 days after a suicidal disclosure by the same student had been formally documented by the institution's Designated Safeguarding Lead. She did not raise the disclosure. She did not request a welfare check. She did not pause the proceedings. This is a failure of the specific duty held by her role.

Finding 4 — Institutional Incoherence (Same-Day Paradox)

The institution requested the student's course feedback on 10 May 2024 and marketed summer opportunities to him on 13 May 2024 — the same day his exclusion letter was issued. The exclusion was not communicated across the institution before it was enacted. The process was not coordinated. It was localised to the Panel.

Finding 5 — Infrastructure Failure Misattributed (Email System)

The institution's Exchange system (ulaw365.onmicrosoft.com) generated documented Undeliverable bounces in January and February 2024. A quarantine filter was known to be misfiring on SharePoint links. The appeal email, sent to six recipients on this system, was attributed to the student as a failure to engage. The email exists in the institution's own archive. The "junk" defence is an infrastructure failure owned by the institution, not the student.

Finding 6 — Audi Altera Partem (The Appeal Was Not Heard)

A formal appeal citing Equality Act s.20(3) and s.20(4), twelve specific policy page references, and direct questions requiring answers was sent to every member of the decision-making panel and the Dean. It existed for 62 days before the exclusion decision. It was dismissed as containing "no relevant information" without recorded reasoning. The questions it posed received no documented response. The right to be heard was not exercised. It was extinguished.

Finding 7 — Safeguarding Compartmentalisation

A suicidal disclosure was made on 15 February 2024 by the Designated Safeguarding Lead — formally, in writing, with referrals to statutory services. This disclosure did not appear in any subsequent academic decision documentation. It did not feature in the Stage 2 meeting. It did not feature in the Stage 3 letter. It was compartmentalised away from the process that ended the student's education. Safeguarding and academic progression were treated as separate systems. They are not.

Seven threads. Seven individuals or systems, each failing at a specific, identifiable point. None of these failures are ambiguous. None require interpretation. Each is documented in timestamped email correspondence, in the institution's own records, in the very archive from which their case was built.

The pattern does not suggest a process that failed. It suggests a process that was never genuinely intended to succeed for this student. The decision preceded the procedure. The procedure was the record.
