PART I — Introduction & Declaration
1. This document constitutes a formal rebuttal of the claims made by the Respondent in the letter dated 13 May 2024, authored by Rachel Edge, Secretary of the Stage 3 Support to Study Panel, and a forensic determination identifying seven previously unacknowledged threads of institutional failure within the email record.
2. This rebuttal is compiled from timestamped email records exported from the Appellant's institutional inbox (Case Ref DC23667, 1,837 pages) and the Appellant's sent-items engagement record (214 pages). Every claim made by the Respondent is set against a specific, dated, quoted piece of evidence. The evidence links contained herein direct to highlighted screenshots of the source pages, hosted at the address set out in Part IV of this document.
3. No claim in this document is unsupported. Where the Appellant's evidence contradicts a finding of the Panel, the contradiction is identified by reference to the specific page, date, and content of the source material.
4. The Appellant states that the exclusion decision of 13 May 2024 was procedurally tainted, that the claims of non-engagement are statistically false, that safeguarding disclosures were compartmentalised away from the decision-making process, and that key members of the Panel held undisclosed conflicts of interest.

PART III — Forensic Determination
17. This Part identifies seven previously unacknowledged evidentiary threads within the email record. Each thread is a discrete chain of causation linking a specific individual or system to a specific failure. The threads do not stand alone. They converge. Taken individually, each is serious. Taken together, they constitute a pattern.

18. On or after 1 February 2024, Brian Lutchmiah, DIS Manager, promised ULIP support. Angele Venchard emailed the Appellant stating: "As promised by the DIS Manager, Brian, we are happy to help going forward" (DC23667 page 640). Action Plan item 3 was to review the ULIP with the DIS advisor — the very support that Brian Lutchmiah had promised. He did not recuse himself. He did not flag the conflict. See Exhibit N.

PART VI — Relief Sought & Closing Statement
27. On the basis of the evidence set out in this document, the Appellant submits that the exclusion decision of 13 May 2024 is vitiated on the following grounds:
28. Finding 1 — Conflict of Interest. Brian Lutchmiah initiated the ULIP support offer and subsequently sat on the panel that declared it was not acted upon, without recusal. The finding on Action Plan item 3 is tainted.
29. Finding 2 — Prior Knowledge. Salome Verrell was CC'd on three support and safeguarding-related emails. The liaison claim is directly contradicted by her own correspondence record and is unsustainable.
30. Finding 3 — Duty of Care Breach. The Mental Health Advisor sat on a panel 20 days after a suicidal disclosure had been formally documented. She did not raise it. This is a failure of the specific duty held by her role.
31. Finding 4 — Institutional Incoherence. The exclusion was not communicated across the institution before it was enacted. The process was localised to the Panel.
32. Finding 5 — Infrastructure Failure Misattributed. The institution's Exchange system was documented as failing in January and February 2024. The "junk" defence is an infrastructure failure owned by the institution, not the student.
33. Finding 6 — Audi Altera Partem. A formal appeal citing Equality Act obligations was sent to every member of the decision-making panel and the Dean. It existed for 62 days before the exclusion decision. It was dismissed without recorded reasoning. The right to be heard was extinguished.
34. Finding 7 — Safeguarding Compartmentalisation. A suicidal disclosure was made on 15 February 2024, formally, in writing, with referrals to statutory services. It did not appear in any subsequent academic decision documentation. Safeguarding and academic progression were treated as separate systems. They are not.
35. The Appellant requests that the exclusion decision be set aside on the ground that it was reached in breach of procedural fairness, in breach of the institution's duty of care, and in breach of the Equality Act 2010. The Appellant further requests that the matter be referred to an independent review panel, that a full safeguarding review be conducted, and that the Appellant's registration be reinstated pending that review.
